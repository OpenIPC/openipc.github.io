## What Is Faceter

Faceter's video surveillance solution combines three layers: hardware, cloud, and analytics.

### Hardware

A lineup of OpenIPC and Faceter cameras: models for everyday tasks and professional-grade devices for sites with higher requirements.
Every model works with the Faceter app right out of the box - no extra NVR, no manual RTSP stream setup.
Third-party cameras are also supported via RTSP and ONVIF.

### Cloud

The platform stores footage archives and provides remote access through a web interface, mobile apps (iOS, Android, Windows, macOS, Android TV), and a Telegram bot.
Hybrid setups are supported too, with part of the footage stored locally on an NVR and part in the cloud.

### Analytics

This is the core of the product: the system detects motion and describes what's happening in plain, human language.
Instead of "Motion detected in Zone 2" users get reports like "A man in a dark jacket crossed the perimeter near the gate" That's the Faceter AI agent at work.


## Who It's For

Faceter serves several segments: businesses (marketplaces, construction sites, cafes, restaurants, offices, property management companies, the auto industry, kindergartens), individual users, and integrators.
An API is available for developers who want to embed video analytics into their own systems or automate response workflows.
A standalone detector module is also available as a separate component of the Faceter ecosystem, for custom integrations.


## Faceter and OpenIPC

The partnership with OpenIPC marks one of the key milestones in Faceter's development. 
Open firmware, independence from any single vendor, and a huge base of compatible cameras made OpenIPC the ideal entry point for our development and deployment team.
Early on, Faceter ran into a basic market paradox: there was plenty of freedom, but no real analytics on the cameras themselves.
Basic motion detection reacted to leaves, shadows, and glare.

OpenIPC's integration and expertise let us solve that problem at the architecture level. We built a hybrid detector designed specifically around the constraints of low-power chipsets.
